# Lesson 11 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through this class you *built* circuits. This last time, you get to **explain** one. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a curious friend.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything worth thinking about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words. I just want to see that you understand what's really going on inside the machine.

---

## The Question

> **Explain how a machine that only understands 0 and 1 can do something as clever as adding two numbers.**

Think about:

- What are gates, and what do they work with?
- How did you turn simple gates into something that *adds* (remember the half adder and full adder)?
- Why does "just 1s and 0s" not mean "too simple to be clever"?

You built this from the ground up — now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. ⚙️
