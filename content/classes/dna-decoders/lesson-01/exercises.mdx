## Your mission

### Exercise 1 — `first_base(dna)`

Give back the **first base** of the strand. Remember: the first spot is `0`.

```python
first_base("ATCGTAGC")   # -> "A"
first_base("GC")         # -> "G"
```

**Hint:** reach into the strand at spot 0 — `dna[0]` — and `return` it.

---

### Exercise 2 — `last_base(dna)`

Give back the **last base** of the strand. You don't have to count to the end — `-1` means the last spot.

```python
last_base("ATCGTAGC")   # -> "C"
last_base("GC")         # -> "C"
```

**Hint:** `dna[-1]` is the very last base, no matter how long the strand is.

When both work, press Run and watch the Graph panel paint your strand. The little square underneath lights up under the base you grabbed — first on the left, last on the right. 🧬
