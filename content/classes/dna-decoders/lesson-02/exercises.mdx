## Your mission

### Exercise 1 — `snip(dna, start, end)`

Give back the **piece** of the strand from spot `start` up to (but **not including**) spot `end`.

```python
snip("ATCGTAGC", 2, 5)   # -> "CGT"   (spots 2, 3, 4)
snip("ATCGTAGC", 0, 3)   # -> "ATC"   (the first three bases)
```

**Hint:** put both spots in the brackets with a colon between them — `dna[start:end]` — and `return` it.

---

### Exercise 2 — `flip(dna)`

Give back the whole strand read **backwards**.

```python
flip("ATCGTAGC")   # -> "CGATGCTA"
flip("GATTACA")    # -> "ACATTAG"
```

**Hint:** `dna[::-1]` walks the strand from the end to the start. That's the whole thing.

When both work, press Run and watch the Graph panel. The bottom row shows the piece you snipped — then tap the **Flip** chip to see the strand reverse. 🧬
