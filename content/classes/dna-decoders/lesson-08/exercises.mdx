# Lesson 8 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through DNA Decoders you *read and decoded* DNA. This last time, you get to **explain** the big idea behind it. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who's never seen DNA.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words and your own examples. I just want to see that you understand the idea.

---

## The Question

> **Why do scientists say DNA is like a written code or a recipe?**

Think about:

- What is DNA actually made of, and why does the *order* of those letters matter?
- How is reading DNA three letters at a time like following instructions? (Remember chunking the genome!)
- What happens to the "instructions" if you change a letter?

You decoded creatures from raw DNA — now put the big idea in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🧬
