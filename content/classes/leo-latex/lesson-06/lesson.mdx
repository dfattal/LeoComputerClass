## Big Idea

In your book so far, every answer has lived on the number line. This is the chapter where the line *bursts*.

You found it on paper through rotation: multiplying by $-1$ spins the number line $180┬░$. So what number gives a **quarter turn** тАФ $90┬░$ тАФ so that doing it *twice* lands on $-1$? No number on the line can. So you stepped *off* the line:

$$i^2 = -1$$

And suddenly numbers have **two coordinates**: $3 + 2i$ is a *point* тАФ three steps east, two steps north. Today you typeset arithmetic in this new plane. And here's the wonderful part: the grader has been a citizen of the complex plane all along. Your checks this week have answers like $-2 + 3i$, and it verifies *both* coordinates.

## The Typography of $i$

No new commands today тАФ $i$ is just the letter `i`. What's new is the *craft* of placing it:

- **Squared:** `i^2` тЖТ $i^2$
- **With a coefficient, $i$ trails:** `3i`, `2i` тЖТ $3i,\ 2i$ тАФ the number of steps first, then the direction
- **As a multiplier out front, $i$ leads:** `i\left(3+2i\right)` тЖТ $i\left(3+2i\right)$

One pitfall: in math mode, $i$ slants like every variable тАФ that's correct here ($i$ *is* a number, not a function). The flinch you trained for upright $\ln$ doesn't apply.

## Three Facts of the New Plane

**The definition, as arithmetic.** $i^2 = i \cdot i = -1$. Two quarter-turns make a half-turn. When the grader checks this chain, it multiplies $i$ by $i$ *as complex numbers* and confirms it lands exactly on $-1 + 0i$.

**Conjugate partners.** Flip the sign of the imaginary part and you get a number's mirror twin: $2+3i$ and $2-3i$. Multiply them тАФ

$$(2+3i)(2-3i) = 4 - 9i^2 = 13$$

тАФ and the $i$'s annihilate (difference of squares, your old friend, with $i^2 = -1$ doing the flip from $-9$ to $+9$). Twin times twin is always plain and real. Remember this trick: in three weeks it summons a circle out of nothing.

**Multiply to rotate.** Take the point $3+2i$ and multiply by $i$:

$$i\left(3+2i\right) = 3i + 2i^2 = -2 + 3i$$

Plot both points. $(3,2)$ became $(-2,3)$ тАФ the *same arrow*, swung exactly $90┬░$ counterclockwise. Multiplication by $i$ **is** rotation. That's not a metaphor; it's what the arithmetic does. Hold onto this feeling тАФ it's the secret engine of Euler's formula next week.

## When the Answer Isn't Real

Until today, a final answer was one number. Now look at exercise 3: the correct answer is $-2 + 3i$ тАФ **two** numbers wearing one coat. The grader checks the real part is $-2$ *and* the imaginary part is $3$. Drop the $i$ term, or get its sign wrong, and it will tell you which coordinate broke.

You're now writing mathematics that lives in two dimensions. Your book just got a whole new direction.
