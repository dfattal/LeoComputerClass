## Exercise 1: Euler's Identity

$$e^{i\pi} = \cos(\pi) + i\sin(\pi) = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `e^{i\pi}` (all of $i\pi$ inside the exponent braces), `\cos`, `\sin`, `\pi`.

**Hints:**

## Exercise 3: The Grand Finale

Under `%% grand-finale`, the area under one arch of the sine wave:

$$\int_0^{\pi} \sin(x)\,dx = \left[\;?\;\right]_0^{\pi} = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `\int_0^{\pi}` ($\pi$ braced in the bound!), `\,` before the $dx$, `\left[ \right]` with bounds, `\sin`, `\cos`.

**Hints:**
- What differentiates to $\sin(x)$? Lesson 7 says $\cos' = -\sin$… so the antiderivative of sine is **minus** cosine. The minus lives *inside* the bracket.
- $-\cos(\pi) - (-\cos(0)) = 1 + 1$. The arch holds exactly $2$.
- This is page one's three-step dance — integral, bracket, answer — danced at the summit.
