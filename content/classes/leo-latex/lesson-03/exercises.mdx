## Exercise 1: The Arrangement Counter

Under `%% factorial`, unroll $5!$ all the way:

$$5! = \;? \cdot ? \cdot ? \cdot ? \cdot ? = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `!` and `\cdot`. At least **3 chain parts**: the factorial, the unrolled product, the value.

**Hints:**
- The factorial symbol comes *after* the number: `5!`.
- Use `\cdot` between every factor — this is the one page where multiplication dots shine.
- The grader computes $5!$ itself, computes your product, computes your final number — all three must agree.

## Exercise 2: Five Choose Two

Under `%% choose`, typeset the full story of $\binom{5}{2}$:

$$\binom{5}{2} = \frac{?!}{?!\;?!} = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `\binom{…}{…}`, `\frac{…}{…}`, `!`. Three chain parts again.

**Hints:**
- `\binom` works exactly like `\frac` — two brace groups — but renders without the bar.
- The formula: $5!$ upstairs; $2!$ times $(5-2)! = 3!$ downstairs.
- Ten ways to pick 2 friends from 5. If your chain says otherwise, the grader will name the step that broke.

## Exercise 3: The Instant Expansion

Under `%% binomial-theorem`, expand without multiplying out:

$$(a+b)^4 = \;?$$

Five terms. Coefficients straight from Pascal's row 4: $1, 4, 6, 4, 1$. Powers of $a$ counting down, powers of $b$ counting up.

**You'll need:** `^` (and the brace rule!). **No `\cdot` allowed** — book style is $4a^3b$, everything snug.

**Hints:**
- Pattern per term: coefficient, then `a^{power}`, then `b^{power}` — like `6a^2b^2`.
- `a^3b` puts only the 3 up. `a^{3b}` is a different claim entirely — and the grader evaluates at several $(a, b)$ pairs with $a \neq b$, so swapped or mangled terms get caught.
- Check the edges: first term all $a$, last term all $b$, coefficients symmetric.
