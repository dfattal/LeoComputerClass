\section{The Art of Counting}

Factorials, choices, and Pascal's triangle, typeset by Leo.

%% factorial
Five things can line up in this many ways:

$$ 5! = 5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1 = 120 $$

%% choose
Picking 2 friends out of 5 — order doesn't matter:

$$ \binom{5}{2} = \frac{5!}{2! \cdot 3!} = 10 $$

%% binomial-theorem
Row 4 of Pascal's triangle, working as algebra:

$$ (a+b)^4 = a^4 + 4a^3b + 6a^2b^2 + 4ab^3 + b^4 $$
