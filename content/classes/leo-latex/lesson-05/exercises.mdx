## Exercise 1: The Undo Button

Under `%% undo-button`, chain both undo directions into one line ending at plain $x$:

$$e^{\ln(x)} = \ln\left(e^x\right) = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `e^{\ln(x)}` (a command inside an exponent's braces), `\ln`, and `\left( \right)` around the $e^x$.

**Hints:**
- The exponent braces hold the whole `\ln(x)` — without them, only the `\` ends up upstairs and chaos follows.
- Three links, all equal: the grader evaluates each at several positive $x$ and demands total agreement.
- Why grow the parens in $\ln\left(e^x\right)$? There's a superscript inside — plain parens would look squashed next to it.

## Exercise 2: The Mirror's Slope

Under `%% mirror-slope`, publish your chain-rule trophy:

$$\frac{d}{dx} \ln(x) = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `\frac{d}{dx}`, `\ln`, and a `\frac` for the answer.

**Hints:**
- You *derived* this: differentiate both sides of $e^{\ln(x)} = x$ with the gear train, and the slope of $\ln$ pops out.
- The answer is the simplest fraction in your book: one over $x$.
- The grader nudges $x$ and measures $\ln$'s actual slope — your fraction must match at every sample.

## Exercise 3: Multiplication Becomes Addition

Under `%% log-law`, typeset the mirror of the exponent rule:

$$\ln(ab) = \;? + \;?$$

**You'll need:** `\ln` (three times!), letters $a$ and $b$.

**Hints:**
- Inside the first $\ln$: just `ab` — touching means multiplied.
- This is a *law*, so the grader tests it at several independent pairs $(a, b)$. Write $\ln(a) \cdot \ln(b)$ instead of the sum and watch it get demolished.
- Feel the mirror: $e^a e^b = e^{a+b}$ says exponents add. Take $\ln$ of everything and… products become sums.
