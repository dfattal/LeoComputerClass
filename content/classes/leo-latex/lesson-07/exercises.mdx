# Lesson 7 — Exercises: The Great Split

## How This Works

One new tool this week: the `align*` block for multi-line derivations. The grader reads the whole block as a single chain — every `&=` line is a link, and every link is verified with real (well… complex) numbers.

---

## Exercise 1: The Great Split

Under `%% the-split`, typeset Euler's formula:

$$e^{ix} = \;? + i\,?$$

**You'll need:** `e^{ix}` (both letters inside the exponent braces!), `\cos`, `\sin`.

**Hints:**
- The real family first, then $i$ times the imaginary family.
- The grader evaluates $e^{ix}$ as a true complex exponential at several $x$ and matches **both coordinates** against your right side. The formula has to be exactly right.
- Feel what it says: as $x$ grows, $e^{ix}$ *walks around a circle* — cosine is its east-west shadow, sine its north-south.

## Exercise 2: Summoning the Circle

Under `%% circle-sum`, multiply the split by its conjugate twin in a proper two-line derivation:

```
$$ \begin{align*}
(the twins, multiplied) &= (what the product expands to) \\
&= (the famous number)
\end{align*} $$
```

**You'll need:** `\begin{align*}`/`\end{align*}`, `&=` on both lines, `\\` between them, `\left( \right)` around each twin, `\cos^2(x)`, `\sin^2(x)`.

**Hints:**
- The twins: $\cos(x) + i\sin(x)$ and $\cos(x) - i\sin(x)$. Side by side, each in grown parens.
- Their product is last week's annihilation: $\cos^2 - i^2\sin^2$, and $i^2 = -1$ flips the minus to a **plus**.
- The last line is the whole unit circle in one digit. The grader checks every link at several $x$ — your derivation's spine, certified.

## Exercise 3: The Sleeping Power Rule

Under `%% sine-slope`, publish the derivative that comes free with the series:

$$\frac{d}{dx} \sin(x) = \;?$$

**You'll need:** `\frac{d}{dx}`, `\sin`, `\cos`.

**Hints:**
- No memorizing — sine is an infinite polynomial now, and polynomials bow to the power rule.
- The grader measures sine's actual slope at its sample points. Only one function matches it everywhere.
