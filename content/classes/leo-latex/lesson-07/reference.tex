\section{The Great Split}

A rotation goes into the tower — and geometry comes out, typeset by Leo.

%% the-split
Feed ix into the exponential series and it splits into two families:

$$ e^{ix} = \cos(x) + i\sin(x) $$

%% circle-sum
Multiply the split by its conjugate — the twins trick from last week:

$$ \begin{align*}
\left(\cos(x) + i\sin(x)\right)\left(\cos(x) - i\sin(x)\right) &= \cos^2(x) + \sin^2(x) \\
&= 1
\end{align*} $$

%% sine-slope
Sine and cosine are now just infinite polynomials — so the power rule runs them:

$$ \frac{d}{dx} \sin(x) = \cos(x) $$
