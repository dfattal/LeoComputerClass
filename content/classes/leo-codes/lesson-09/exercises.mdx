# Lesson 9 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through Secret Codes you *built and broke* ciphers. This last time, you get to **explain** the cleverest one. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a fellow spy-in-training.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words and your own examples (the color-mixing paint is a great one!). I just want to see that you understand the trick.

---

## The Question

> **How can two people agree on a secret code over the internet even if a spy hears everything they say?**

Think about:

- What's the big problem with normal codes when the two people are far apart?
- Remember the color-mixing trick — what makes it work even when everyone is watching? (Easy to mix, impossible to un-mix.)
- Why can't the spy figure out the final secret, even though they heard every message?

You built this yourself — now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🔐
