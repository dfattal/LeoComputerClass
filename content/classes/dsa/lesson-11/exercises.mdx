## One last reflection

No code editor this time — just a box for your words on the right. Take your time and write a few honest sentences. Your coach will read them and talk it through with you.

**The question:** Two programs do the exact same job, but one finishes in a blink and the other takes an age. Why can the *same* job take such wildly different amounts of time — and how do you pick the tool that makes it fast?

Think about:

- **What Big-O really measures.** It's not "how fast is the computer" — it's how the *number of steps* grows as the job gets bigger. Explain that in your own words.
- **The shapes you drew.** Flat (O(1)), barely-rising (O(log n)), straight (O(n)), steep hill (O(n²)), gentle dive (O(n log n)). Which beat which, and what did each one cost?
- **A real example from your own code.** A time when choosing a better tool or structure — sorting first so you could binary-search, swapping bubble sort for merge sort, or using a hash table — made the work shrink.
- **Where you've seen it elsewhere.** DNS caching in Networks, password length in White Hat, the scheduler in Operating Systems.

When you're ready, click **Submit answer**. There's no single right wording — explain it like you'd explain it to a friend.
