# Lesson 9 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through Space School you *simulated* orbits. This last time, you get to **explain** the big idea behind them. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend looking up at the Moon.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words. I just want to see that you truly understand what an orbit is.

---

## The Question

> **Why does the Moon keep circling Earth instead of crashing into it, even though it's always falling?**

Think about:

- Is the Moon actually being pulled by Earth's gravity, or is it floating free?
- What *else* is the Moon doing besides falling?
- How do those two things combine so it falls forever without ever landing (and without flying away)?

You built this exact thing in code — now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🌙
