# Lesson 8 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

For your whole journey you taught the **computer** what to do. This last time, *you* get to do the explaining. There's no Python here and nothing to Run. Just a question, and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who's never coded.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back to tell you what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words, your own examples — even a silly one. I just want to see that *you get it.*

---

## The Question

> **How can a screen show a colorful picture using only a grid of tiny squares and numbers?**

Think about:

- What is each tiny square called, and what does it hold?
- How does a smooth-looking photo get turned into something a computer can store or send?
- Why don't we *see* the little squares when we look at a normal screen?

Take your time. When you're happy with your explanation, hit **Submit**. 🎨
