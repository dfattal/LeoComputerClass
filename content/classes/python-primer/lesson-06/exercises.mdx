# Lesson 6 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through this class you wrote instructions for the **computer**. This last time, *you* do the explaining. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who's never coded.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words and your own examples. I just want to see that *you understand the idea.*

---

## The Question

> **Why did people invent programming languages like Python instead of just talking to computers in plain English?**

Think about:

- What does a computer *actually* understand at the very bottom?
- Why is plain English a problem for a computer that can't guess what you mean?
- How does a language like Python sit "in the middle" between a human and the machine?

Take your time. When your explanation feels right, hit **Submit**. 🐍
