# Lesson 9 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through White Hat you *broke into* systems and then *defended* them. This last time, you get to **explain** the big idea behind it all. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who thinks all hackers are bad guys.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words. I just want to see that you truly understand what it means to be a white hat.

---

## The Question

> **Why is computer security a never-ending game of "cat and mouse" — and what is the one thing that makes someone a white-hat hacker instead of a crook?**

Think about:

- When defenders fix a hole (like salting passwords or checking a buffer), what do attackers do next?
- Why can't anyone ever just "finish" securing a system once and for all?
- The buffer overflow you wrote is the same code whether it's a crime or a job — so what actually decides which one it is?

You lived both sides this term — the attacker and the defender. Now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🎩
