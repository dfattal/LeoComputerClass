## Hacking the human: social engineering

You can have the strongest password and the best encryption in the world — and it all falls apart if someone tricks *you* into typing your password into the wrong place. Fooling a person instead of a machine is called **social engineering**, and its most common form is **phishing**: a fake email with a fake link, dressed up to look exactly like the real thing.

A phishing email wants you to panic and click before you think. Your job as a white hat is to build the calm, careful checker that *doesn't* panic. Two giveaways you can teach to code:

## Giveaway #1: the look-alike link 🔗

The real site is `fortknocks.com`. A phisher registers something that *looks* almost identical:

```text
https://fortknocks.com.evil.ru/login     <- the real domain is evil.ru!
https://fortkn0cks.com/login             <- that's a ZERO, not an "o"
```

The trick is to read the **whole** domain, not just check that "fortknocks" appears somewhere. The domain is the part right after `https://`, up to the first `/`:

```python
domain_of("https://fortknocks.com.evil.ru/login")   # -> "fortknocks.com.evil.ru"
is_fake_link("https://fortknocks.com.evil.ru/login", "fortknocks.com")  # -> True
```

If that domain isn't *exactly* `fortknocks.com`, it's a fake. 🚩

## Giveaway #2: scary, urgent words 😱

Real companies don't scream at you. Phishers do — because panic makes you stop thinking:

> **URGENT!** Your account will be **SUSPENDED**! **VERIFY** your **PASSWORD NOW** or lose access — **CLICK** here!

Count the alarm words. One or two might be innocent, but a message *stuffed* with `urgent`, `verify`, `password`, `suspended`, `now`, `click` is waving a giant red flag.

## Put it together 🕵️

It's phishing if **either** giveaway shows up — a fake link, **or** a message packed with at least two alarm words:

```python
is_phishing("https://fortknocks.com.evil.ru/x", "Welcome back.", "fortknocks.com")
# -> True   (the link is fake)

is_phishing("https://fortknocks.com/help", "URGENT: verify your password now!", "fortknocks.com")
# -> True   (real link, but the message is a scam)

is_phishing("https://fortknocks.com/login", "Here is your monthly summary.", "fortknocks.com")
# -> False  (real link, calm message — safe!)
```

That middle one is the sneaky case: the link is *real*, but the words give it away. A good detector catches both. 🎯
