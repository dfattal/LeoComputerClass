# Lesson 11 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through Bio Lab you *read, decoded, and edited* DNA. This last time, you get to **explain** the big idea. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who's never taken a biology class.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words and your own examples. I just want to see that you understand the idea.

---

## The Question

> **Explain why changing just one letter in a creature's DNA can change something about it.**

Think about:

- What do a creature's DNA letters actually get turned into? (Remember the chain: DNA → RNA → protein → trait.)
- How is DNA read — does the order of the letters matter?
- How can one tiny change at the start ripple out into something you can *see*?

You made mutations and watched traits change — now put the reason in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🧬
