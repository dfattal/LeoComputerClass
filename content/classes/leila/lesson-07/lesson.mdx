# Decode Creature Traits: Your Genome Secret Decoder Ring

In real life, your DNA is like a massive instruction manual. Different sections of the manual tell your body how to build different things — what color your eyes should be, how tall you'll grow, whether your hair is curly or straight.

Scientists call these sections **genes**. Each gene is a chunk of DNA that codes for a specific **trait**.

## Our Toy Genome

Real genomes are billions of letters long (yours has about 3 billion!). That's way too many to work with, so we're going to use a tiny **toy genome** — just 9 letters long.

Our toy genome is split into three **gene regions**, and each region controls one trait:

| Positions | Trait      | AAA    | GGG    | CCC    | TTT     |
|-----------|-----------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| 0 - 2     | Fur color | white  | black  | brown  | golden  |
| 3 - 5     | Eye color | blue   | green  | brown  | red     |
| 6 - 8     | Tail type | long   | short  | curly  | fluffy  |

Think of it like a **secret decoder ring** for creatures! You look at 3 letters, check the table, and discover what trait they code for.

## How to Read a Genome

Let's decode the genome `GGGCCCAAA`:

1. **Positions 0-2:** `GGG` — look up the fur table — that's **black** fur!
2. **Positions 3-5:** `CCC` — look up the eye table — that's **brown** eyes!
3. **Positions 6-8:** `AAA` — look up the tail table — that's a **long** tail!

So this creature has black fur, brown eyes, and a long tail. Cool, right?

## Dictionaries to the Rescue

In Python, we can store each code table as a **dictionary** — a way to look up values by their key. For example:

```python
FUR_CODES = {"AAA": "white", "GGG": "black", "CCC": "brown", "TTT": "golden"}
```

Then `FUR_CODES["GGG"]` gives you `"black"`. Easy!
