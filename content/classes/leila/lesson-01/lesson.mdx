# Count the DNA Bases

## What Is DNA?

Every living thing -- from tiny bacteria to giant blue whales to **you** -- has something called **DNA** inside its cells. DNA is like a **recipe book** that tells your body how to build and run itself.

But here's the cool part: this recipe book is written in a **4-letter alphabet**!

## The 4 Letters of DNA

The four letters (scientists call them **bases**) are:

- **A** -- Adenine
- **T** -- Thymine
- **C** -- Cytosine
- **G** -- Guanine

That's it! Just four letters. Your entire body -- your eye color, your hair, how tall you'll grow -- is all written using just **A, T, C, and G**.

A tiny piece of DNA might look like this:

```
AATCGGTTACGCTA
```

It's like a secret code that only your cells know how to read!

## Why Count Bases?

Real scientists count DNA bases all the time. It helps them figure out things like:

- **What genes do** -- different patterns of bases have different jobs
- **How species are related** -- your DNA is 98% the same as a chimpanzee's!
- **How to fight diseases** -- some diseases are caused by changes in just one base
