# Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through this class you *built* the internet, piece by piece. This last time, you get to **explain** the whole thing. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who just asked "wait, how does the internet actually work?"
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words. I just want to see that you truly understand the journey you built.

---

## The Question

> **What REALLY happens, step by step, when you type a web address and press enter — how does the page actually get to you?**

Think about:

- You typed a *name*, but machines need a *number*. What turns one into the other?
- How does your request actually travel across the world — in one piece, or lots of little ones? How does each piece find its way?
- Once you reach the server, how do you *ask* for the page, and how does it come back without missing parts?

You built every single one of these steps. Now put the whole journey in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🌍
