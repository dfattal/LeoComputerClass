# Lesson 9 — Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through Motion Lab you *ran* simulations. This last time, you get to **explain** the idea behind them. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who hasn't taken the class.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words and your own examples. I just want to see that you understand *why* the trick works.

---

## The Question

> **Why does chopping motion into tiny time steps let us predict where a Nerf dart will land?**

Think about:

- What do you do to position and velocity on each tiny step?
- Why does pretending the speed is constant for one *tiny* moment work — and why do smaller steps give a better answer?
- Why is this trick so useful when there's air resistance and no neat formula exists?

You used this every single lesson — now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 🎯
