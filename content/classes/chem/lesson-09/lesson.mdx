## The one giant idea

> **Everything is made of the same tiny atoms. The only thing that changes is how they're arranged.**

A diamond and a pencil's lead are *both* just carbon atoms — arranged differently. Ice and steam are the *same* water molecules — spaced differently. A salt crystal is just two kinds of atom, taking turns, copied a billion times.

Nothing in your whole kitchen is truly "new stuff." It's atoms — a few dozen kinds — snapped together, pulled apart, and stacked up in different ways. That's the entire secret of chemistry.

---

## It took humans a very long time to believe this

For thousands of years, people thought there must be hundreds of totally different *substances*, each its own magic material. The idea that everything is just a handful of atom-types, rearranged, sounded crazy.

A scientist named **John Dalton** (early 1800s) was one of the first to argue it seriously: that each element is made of identical tiny atoms, and that reactions just shuffle them around. Later, **Dmitri Mendeleev** lined the elements up by their families and discovered the **periodic table** — the same repeating pattern *you* drew. Piece by piece, scientists realized the whole, dazzling, complicated world is built from a small set of simple pieces.

And you just rebuilt that whole story — in code, from the atom up.
