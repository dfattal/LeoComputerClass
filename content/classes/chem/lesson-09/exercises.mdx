# Your Final Question

## This one is different — no code!

All through Kitchen Chemistry you *built* things — atoms, molecules, reactions, a crystal. This last time, you get to **explain** the big idea behind all of it. There's no Python here and nothing to Run — just a question and a box to write your answer in.

---

## How it works

1. Read the question below.
2. In the answer box on the right, **write your answer in a few sentences** — like you're explaining it to a friend who's never done chemistry.
3. Click **Submit answer**. Your coach will read it and write back about what you nailed and anything to think about more.

There's **no single right wording.** Use your own words. I just want to see that you really understand the big idea.

## The Question

> **A salt crystal is big and sparkly, but it's really just tiny atoms doing simple things. Tell the story of how you get from a single atom all the way up to a whole crystal.**

Think about:

- What is an atom made of, and what decides how it behaves?
- How do atoms join into molecules, and how do molecules stack up?
- What's the one big idea — about atoms and *arranging* — that ties the whole class together?

You built every step of this yourself — now put it in your own words. When it feels right, hit **Submit**. 💎
