# Reactions = Rearranging

> Welcome to Phase 2, {{FIRST_NAME}}! 🎉 You can build atoms, families, molecules, and states. Now for the part everyone calls "chemistry": **reactions** — when stuff changes into *new* stuff. The big secret? A reaction never really makes or destroys anything. It just **rearranges** the atoms you already had.

## Phase 2: Reactions You Can See

## Hydrogen + oxygen → water

Here's a real reaction. Take hydrogen gas and oxygen gas, add a spark, and you get **water**:

```text
2 H₂  +  O₂   →   2 H₂O
```

Read it like a recipe: **2** hydrogen molecules plus **1** oxygen molecule make **2** water molecules. The little arrow means "turns into."

## Count the atoms — nothing escapes

Now the magic. Let's count every atom on each side.

**Before** the arrow:
- Hydrogen: 2 molecules × 2 atoms = **4 H**
- Oxygen: 1 molecule × 2 atoms = **2 O**

**After** the arrow:
- Water: 2 molecules × 2 hydrogens = **4 H**, and 2 molecules × 1 oxygen = **2 O**

**4 H and 2 O on both sides!** The atoms didn't vanish or multiply — they just snapped together in a new way. This always-true rule is called **conservation of mass**. ⚖️

## Counting with code

```python
atoms_of(2, 2)   # -> 4    2 molecules of H₂, 2 atoms each
atoms_of(1, 2)   # -> 2    1 molecule of O₂, 2 atoms
is_balanced(4, 4)  # -> True   same before and after = balanced!
is_balanced(4, 2)  # -> False  uh oh, atoms went missing
```
