# Mixing Colors & Chromatography

> Here's a secret, {{FIRST_NAME}}: a black marker isn't really black. 🖊️ It's a sneaky *mixture* of colored pigments hiding together. Today you'll pull them apart and catch every hidden color — using nothing but paper and water. Scientists call this trick **chromatography**, and it's how labs un-mix almost anything.

## Phase 2: Reactions You Can See

## The race up the paper

Dab a dot of marker near the bottom of a paper strip, then let water slowly creep up. As the water passes the dot, it grabs the pigments and carries them along — like swimmers in a current.

But here's the thing: **each pigment moves at its own speed.** Some are light and zip up fast; others are heavy and lag behind. So after a while, they've spread out into separate **bands** of color. One dot becomes a rainbow! 🌈

## Speed × time = how far

How far up has a pigment climbed? Just its **speed** times how long it's been traveling:

```python
travel(3, 2)   # -> 6   a fast pigment, 2 steps later
travel(1, 2)   # -> 2   a slow pigment, same 2 steps
```

There's one catch: the paper is only **7 squares tall**, so a pigment can't climb off the top. We cap it at 7:

```python
travel(3, 5)   # -> 7   (would be 15, but the paper isn't that tall!)
```

And if you want to know which pigment is winning the race:

```python
highest([1, 2, 3], 2)   # -> 6   the speed-3 pigment is highest
```

## Watch them separate

Press **Run** and step through the three chips:

⚫ **Start** → all pigments dabbed together at the bottom · ⬆️ **Climbing** → they begin to pull apart · 🌈 **Separated** → three clean bands

Fast yellow ends up near the top, slow blue stays low — exactly like a real lab strip.

## What you'll build

- **`travel(speed, time)`** — how far a pigment climbed (capped at 7).
- **`highest(speeds, time)`** — how high the fastest pigment got.
