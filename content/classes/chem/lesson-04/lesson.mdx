# Solid, Liquid, Gas

> Here's something wild, {{FIRST_NAME}}: ice, water, and steam are all *exactly the same stuff.* 🧊💧☁️ Same molecules, every time. The only difference is how much **energy** they have. Today you'll heat a block of particles and watch it melt and boil — without ever changing what it's made of.

## Phase 1: What Everything Is Made Of

---

## Energy = how much the particles jiggle

Every particle is always jiggling a little. **Heat** is just extra jiggle. The more energy you add, the more they bounce — and the further apart they push:

- **Cold** → barely jiggling, packed into a tight, neat block. That's a **solid** (like ice). 🧊
- **Warm** → jiggling more, loose enough to slide past each other. That's a **liquid** (like water). 💧
- **Hot** → jiggling like crazy, flying apart with big gaps. That's a **gas** (like steam). ☁️

## Nothing is created or destroyed

Here's the part scientists love: when ice melts into water, **not a single particle disappears.** It's the *same* nine particles whether they're frozen, melted, or boiled — they just spread out. Watch for that in the panel: the block always has the same number of dots.

## From temperature to spacing

We'll measure heat as a **temperature** from 0 (freezing) to 100 (boiling). Your job is to turn that into a **gap** between particles:

```python
spacing(20)    # -> 0    cold: packed tight
spacing(50)    # -> 1    warm: a little room
spacing(90)    # -> 2    hot: lots of space
```

And then name the state:

```python
state_name(20)   # -> "solid"
state_name(90)   # -> "gas"
```
