## The big idea: what's inside an atom?

An atom has two parts:

- A **nucleus** in the very middle — a tight little clump of **protons**. The number of protons is the atom's secret name tag: 1 proton makes **hydrogen**, 6 makes **carbon**, 8 makes **oxygen**. Change the protons and you change what kind of stuff it is!
- A swarm of **electrons** zooming around the nucleus, way out in rings called **shells**.

Picture the nucleus as the Sun, and the electrons as tiny planets looping around it. 🌞🪐

## Rule 1: a neutral atom is balanced

Protons have a tiny **+** charge and electrons have a tiny **−** charge. In a normal, calm atom they balance out perfectly — so the number of electrons is **exactly the same** as the number of protons.

```python
# An atom with 6 protons (carbon) has...
electron_count(6)   # -> 6 electrons
```

Easy: however many protons, that many electrons.

## Rule 2: electrons stack in shells

The electrons don't all pile into one ring. They fill up **shells**, one at a time, from the inside out:

- The **first shell** holds up to **2** electrons.
- The **next shell** holds up to **8**.

So you fill the inner shell first, and only when it's full do electrons spill into the next one. Carbon has 6 electrons, so it goes **2 in the first shell, 4 in the next**:

```python
shells(6)   # -> [2, 4]
```

The list `[2, 4]` means "2 electrons in shell 1, 4 in shell 2." Neon has 10 electrons, so both shells fill right up: `[2, 8]`.

## See your atom

When you press **Run**, the panel draws your atom for you:

🔴 the **red** square in the middle is the nucleus · 🔵 each **blue** dot is an electron in an inner shell · 🟡 the **yellow** dots are the electrons in the *outer* shell

Those yellow outer electrons get their own color for a reason: they're the ones that bump into other atoms and decide how the atom behaves. You'll do amazing things with them next lesson!

There are two steps you can flip between: first the electrons you **counted**, then the finished **atom** with them ringed into shells — 2 blue inside, 4 yellow around the outside. Watch carbon come to life! ⚛️
