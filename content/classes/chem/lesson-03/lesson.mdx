## Atoms want a full outer shell

Remember the outer shell from last lesson? Here's the thing: atoms *really* want that outer shell to be **full**. Most atoms feel full with **8** electrons out there. Tiny hydrogen is the exception — it's happy with just **2**.

An atom that isn't full will grab onto another atom and **share** electrons to top itself up. A shared pair of electrons is called a **bond** — picture it as a little stick connecting two atoms. 🔗

## How many bonds?

The number of bonds an atom makes is simply **how many more electrons it needs**:

```python
bonds_needed(1, 2)   # -> 1   hydrogen: has 1 outer, wants 2
bonds_needed(6, 8)   # -> 2   oxygen: has 6, wants 8 -> shares 2 (a double bond!)
bonds_needed(4, 8)   # -> 4   carbon: has 4, wants 8 -> makes 4 bonds
```

When two atoms share **two** pairs, that's a **double bond** — a stronger, two-stick connection.

## Counting a molecule

A molecule is just a group of atoms. **Water** is 2 hydrogens + 1 oxygen, which we write as `H₂O`. To count all its atoms, add them up:

```python
molecule_atoms([2, 1])   # -> 3   water: 2 H + 1 O
molecule_atoms([1, 2])   # -> 3   carbon dioxide: 1 C + 2 O
```
